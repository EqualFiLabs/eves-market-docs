## What the pool stack is

The EveUSD pairing pool is an optional, opt-in capital layer that is distinct from the base eveUSDC collateral. It is deployed only when an eveUSD stack is requested. It accepts volatile collateral, today WETH, and on deposit mints two paired tokens that together reclaim that collateral.

| Token | Standard | Decimals | Role |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| eveUSD | ERC20 | 18 | senior, stable-claim tranche |
| EveRiskShares | ERC1155 | n/a | junior, first-loss risk tranche |

Do not confuse eveUSD with eveUSDC. eveUSDC is the USDC wrapper that is the default market collateral. eveUSD is a senior tranche minted only by the pool against WETH.

## Paired minting and recombination

Every deposit mints an equal amount of eveUSD and risk shares into the same active series. Every recombination burns an equal amount of both and returns collateral. The two tokens are strictly paired: one unit of eveUSD always needs one risk share to exit back to collateral.

```solidity deposit
// ETH into the WETH profile, minting a paired tranche
(uint256 seriesId, uint256 eveUSDMinted, uint256 sharesMinted) =
    EveUSDRouter(router).depositETH{value: msg.value}(
        eveUSDReceiver, shareReceiver, minEveUSD, minShares
    );
// sharesMinted == eveUSDMinted, both in the profile's active series
```

Recombining requires returning the matching risk shares alongside the eveUSD. There is no path that redeems eveUSD alone while the risk shares remain outstanding.

## Collateral profiles and series

Each profile has one active series at a time. A profile pins a collateral token, an oracle, a collateral ratio, a price band, fees, and an insurance target. Deposits always target the active series. When a series appreciates or enters recovery, the pool opens a successor series and moves the active pointer, so new deposits never mix with an already-resolved tranche.

The deploy registers eveUSD as collateral profile 1 when eveUSD markets are enabled. That profile is separate from the base eveUSDC profile used by default markets.

## Overcollateralization and the price band

Deposits are overcollateralized according to the profile's `collateralRatioBps`, bounded between 10001 and 30000 basis points. A higher ratio means more collateral is locked per unit of eveUSD minted, giving the senior tranche a buffer before it is impaired. The price band controls when a series rolls over or enters recovery as the collateral mark moves.

## Insurance reserve

Every profile custodies an insurance reserve in raw collateral. A configurable `insuranceTargetBps` sets the desired reserve relative to senior outstanding, and an `insuranceFeeBps` skims a deposit contribution toward that target. Anyone can top up insurance directly.

| View | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| `insuranceReserve(profileId)` | current reserve held |
| `insuranceTarget(profileId)` | desired reserve in collateral terms |
| `insuranceDeficit(profileId)` | shortfall of reserve below target |

The reserve is drawn first when a senior shortfall is realized at recovery finalization, before any loss is socialized.

## Recovery and rollover

If a series' collateral mark falls enough to threaten the senior tranche, the pool enters a timelocked recovery flow. Risk holders can return shares to make the series whole; if they do not, the pool finalizes recovery, draws insurance, and opens a successor series. Conversely, an appreciated series can be rolled over into a successor that carries the upside forward.

Recovery status, claim modes, and operator-assisted recovery flows all live on the pool contract. Integrations should read the series status before displaying whether eveUSD of a given series is fully backed.

## Router convenience

EveUSDRouter wraps the pool for the WETH profile. It accepts native ETH or WETH, handles approval, deposits, and recombination to ETH or WETH, and asserts it holds no residual balances afterward. It is a user-facing helper and holds no special privilege over the pool.
